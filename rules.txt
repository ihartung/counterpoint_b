Assumptions: 
1. There must be movement between nodes, i.e. one cannot repeat the previous interval.
2. Direct motion includes movement in the same direction by dissimilar steps.

Chapter 1:
1. From one perfect consonance to another perfect consonance one must proceed in oblique or contrary motion
2. From a perfect consonance to an imperfect consonance one may proceed in any of the three
3. From an imperfect consonance to a perfect consonance one must proceed in oblique or contrary motion
4. From an imperfect consonance to another imperfect consonance one may proceed in any of the three.
Chapter 2:
1. The beginning and end must be perfect consonances.
2. The penultimate bar must be a major sixth if the cantus firmus is in the lower part, a minor third if it is in the upper part
